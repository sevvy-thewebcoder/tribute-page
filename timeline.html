Time-Line

A Tribute to Late. Steve Jobs

Here's a time - line of the life of Steve Jobs:

  • 1955 -

    Born in San Francisco, California, US.

  • 1968 -

    Steve was 13 yrs old, when he was given a summer job by Bill Hewlett (of Hewlett-Packard) after Steve cold-called him to ask for parts for an electronics project.

    In the same year, Steve began his first year study at Homestead High School in Los Altos.

  • 1972 -

    Steve enrolled at Reed College in Portland, Oregon.

    After just one semester, Steve dropped out of Reed College without telling his parents. Steve later explained that he decided to drop out because he did not want to spend his parents' money on an education that seemed meaningless to him.

  • 1976 -

    In this year, Steve, his friend- Wozniak, and administrative overseer Ronald Wayne founded Apple Computer Company (now called Apple Inc.) as a business partnership in Jobs's parents' Crist Drive home on April 1, 1976.

  • 1984 -

    Steve, introduced the Macintosh to a wildly enthusiastic audience at Apple's annual shareholders meeting held in the Flint Auditorium; The Macintosh was based on The Lisa (and Xerox PARC's mouse-driven graphical user interface), and it was widely acclaimed by the media with strong initial sales supporting it.

  • 1985 -

    Steve, left the Apple Computer Company after the faliure of Macintosh.

    Following his resignation from Apple in 1985, Jobs founded NeXT Inc. with $7 million.

  • 1991 -

    On March 18, 1991, Steve married to Laurene Powell.

  • 1997 -

    Steve returned to Apple, after Apple bought NeXT Inc. for $427 million

  • 1998 -

    Apple iMac G3 was introduced in 1998 and its innovative design was directly the result of Steve's return to Apple.

  • 2001 -

    Apple introduced two new products: iTunes and iPod.

  • 2003 -

    Steve was diagnosed with cancer. In mid-2004, he announced to his employees that he had a cancerous tumor in his pancreas.

  • 2007 -

    Steve launched his most ambitious project: Apple iPhone.

  • 2010 -

    In 2010, Apple designed a Tablet Computer and launched the product with name: iPad

  • 2011 -

    On August 24, 2011, Steve announced his resignation as Apple's CEO, writing to the board,
    "I have always said if there ever came a day when I could no longer meet my duties and expectations as Apple's CEO, I would be the first to let you know. Unfortunately, that day has come." Steve became chairman of the board and named Tim Cook as his successor as CEO.

    Steve died at the age of 56 at his Palo Alto, California home on October 5, 2011, due to complications from a relapse of his previously treated islet-cell pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor, which resulted in respiratory arrest.

"Steve was my hero growing up. He not only gave me a lot of personal advice and encouragement, he showed all of us how innovation can change lives. I will miss him dearly, as will the world."

-- Jerry Yang, Yahoo!, Inc. co-founder and former CEO
